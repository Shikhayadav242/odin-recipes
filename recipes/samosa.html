Samosa Recipe


Ingredients


  • For the dough (outer covering):
    • All-purpose flour (maida) : 2 cups
    • Carom seeds (ajwain) : ½ tsp
    • Salt : ½ tsp
    • Oil / ghee: 4 tbsp (for moyan, helps make crust crispy)
    • Water: as needed (to knead a stiff dough)
  • For the filling:
    • Potatoes: 4 medium (boiled & mashed roughly)
    • Green peas: ½ cup (boiled)
    • Green chili: 1 (finely chopped, optional)
    • Ginger: 1 tsp (grated)
    • Cumin seeds: 1 tsp
    • Coriander powder: 1 tsp
    • Garam masala: 1 tsp
    • Red chili powder: ½ tsp
    • Amchur(dry mango powder): ½ tsp (optional, for tanginess)
    • Salt: to taste
    • Oil: 2 tbsp (for cooking filling)
    • Fresh coriander leaves: 2 tbsp (chopped)

STEPS


  1. For the dough:
    • In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, carom seeds, salt, and oil/ghee.
    • Mix well and gradually add water to knead a stiff dough.
    • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Make the filling
    • Add boiled and mashed potatoes, green peas, green chili, ginger, cumin seeds, coriander powder, garam masala, red chili powder, amchur, and salt.
    • Heat oil in a pan, add the filling mixture, and cook for a few minutes.
    • Remove from heat and let it cool. Add fresh coriander leaves.
  3. Shape the samosas
    • Divide dough into equal balls. Roll each ball into an oval (about 6 inches).
    • Cut the oval in half to form two semi-circles.
    • Take one semi-circle, apply water on the straight edge, and fold it to form a cone.
    • Fill the cone with the potato filling and seal the open edge with water.
    • Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.
  4. Fry the samosas
    • Heat oil in a deep pan on medium-low flame.
    • Add samosas in small batches.
    • Fry slowly until golden brown and crisp (about 10–12 minutes per batch).
  5. Garnish & serve
    • Drain on tissue paper.
    • Serve hot with green chutney, tamarind chutney, or ketchup.

TIPS!

  • Use stiff, rested dough : makes rolling easier and gives a crispy, flaky crust.
  • Cool the filling before stuffing : prevents dough from tearing and keeps samosas intact.
  • Fry on medium-low heat : ensures even cooking and a golden, crispy exterior.
